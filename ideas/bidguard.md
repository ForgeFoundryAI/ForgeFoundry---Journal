# BidGuard

**The Combined Evolution**  
**Final Score:** 91.4/100 (Foundry Level)

### Origin Story
BidGuard was born from two strong independent sparks discovered by Crucible:

- **BidLite** (Score: 84.7/100)  
  Lightweight estimating & bidding tool for solo contractors. Focused on turning site photos + inputs into professional quotes fast, without enterprise bloat.

- **ScopeGuard** (Score: 89.2/100)  
  In-job scope protection and change order tool. Solved the painful “after the bid is won” profit leakage from undocumented client requests and scope creep.

### The Merge Moment
During a Spark scan, Crucible identified that these two ideas were highly complementary. Instead of treating them separately, the **Evolution Layer** combined them into one cohesive end-to-end solution: a single tool that follows the contractor from winning the bid all the way through job completion and payment protection.

This was the first major real-world example of the Evolution Layer working as intended — turning two good sparks into something significantly stronger.

### Core Value Proposition
A dead-simple, mobile-first app for solo contractors that:
- Starts with fast, professional bidding (BidLite strength)
- Seamlessly continues into job execution with real-time scope protection and change order handling (ScopeGuard strength)
- Protects profit at every stage of the job

### Evolution Log

- **Initial Discovery**: BidLite and ScopeGuard surfaced separately in different Crucible scans.
- **Evolution Trigger**: User prompted combination exploration → Crucible identified natural synergy (pre-job + in-job protection).
- **Forge Stage**: Refined into a unified workflow with shared dashboard, consistent UI, and end-to-end data flow.
- **Current Status**: Strong Foundry candidate. Ready for full Blueprint development.

**Why This Matters**  
BidGuard is the first living example of ForgeFoundry’s full promise:  
**Spark → Forge (evolution) → Foundry (completion)** — all happening transparently inside the same system.

This is exactly the kind of “build the machine while using the machine” moment we’re documenting.
